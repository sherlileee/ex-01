The Invisible Interaction

Key words:

Social relationship, behavior, mediation, interaction, invisible, moments

Interior Research Question:

How do the programmatic moments mediate people’s perception of space and enhance their interaction with the surrounding?

In maintaining space as a ‘social construct’ , this research examines the invisible relationships between individuals and their environment. Through diagramming a series of moments that incite interaction, it investigates how people perceive space through arranged programs and how these programs are constructed in a mediating situation to encourage interactions. The research aim to understand how design can influence social behavior, the perception of personal identity and the experience of communication within a spatial environment. The projects engage with interventions into social spaces, by using a range of materials and scales in order to explore how people become more aware or connected to their environments and their surrounding people. By making people more aware of their own self within these social environments, the mediated situations emerge - shifting the relationship between people and space, and people and people.

FUTURE CANGGU

In a month of working with companies – CushCush Bali and ARCC Architecture, I was working as a group with Crystal, and we have been asked to propose a design for future Canggu. Our process of work was to make a journal about our daily exploration while exploring Bali city. Then we started to incorporate the experiences together and started to make a proposed design for the assigned place. In the end, we installed our works in an exhibition as a way to communicate our result to the others. Started by analyzing and visiting around Bali, I have the chance to get the senses of Bali culture and Bali people lifestyle, and those senses became the main theme in the design. I have collected and drawn down my each day’s experiences into diagrams, and they become my portrayal and memories of Bali internship.

3 6 5 0 F M E

The project explores the possibilities of the exhibition space as an active site for participation. It looks at the cultures of display that influence exhibition spaces and investigates strategies employed by exhibition designers to frame the objects and ideas in their care in order to communicate their importance and meaning to the different participating publics. The course encourages me to be agents of change - with aims to provoking active engagement and sensory participation in artworks and collections.

Take A Walk In Your Office

The project is involve refitting an existing building with an office design to suit the workplace of tomorrow. As part of the studio, I have explored the notion of ‘office furniture’ and I have produced a prototype ‘element’ of my office design. For the ‘element’, there are a huge variely of office models available to businesses, but it is the role of the designer to customise a office environment to their clients programmatic needs as well as the clients philosophies.

Little UTOPIA In Big Spaces

Little Utopias In Big Spaces consists of a travel studio in Berlin for fifteen days at the AEDES campus, ANCB Metropolitan Laboratory, and a four- week studio in RMIT campus. It is part of a larger international collaboration coordinated by the ANCB. Six universities from USA, Canada, Australia and Chile are involved in the collaboractive research: FUTURE URBANSCAPE summer 2015- 2016. It aims to develop an understanding of the Kultureforum and explore possibilities for the future urban planning and design in the context of participatory urban planning.
